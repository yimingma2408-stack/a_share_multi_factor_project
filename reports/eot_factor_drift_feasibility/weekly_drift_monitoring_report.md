# Weekly Drift Monitoring Diagnostics

## Judgment

Weekly EOT drift is better interpreted as a **contemporaneous factor instability indicator** than as a strong predictive warning for future factor returns.

The factors with the most negative average high-minus-normal 4-week long-short difference are momentum_3m, volatility_1m. Even there, the relationship is not uniformly negative across horizons and IC measures.

High drift states are useful dashboard warnings because they identify unusual changes in the joint distribution of Rank IC, long-short return, and downside return. They should trigger review and a bounded risk penalty, not an automatic factor shutdown.

The downside probability is defined as the probability that the mean of the next four weekly long-short returns is negative. Worst-quantile columns report the 10th percentile.
